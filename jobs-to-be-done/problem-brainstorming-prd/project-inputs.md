# Required Inputs (Not Included)

This project assumes access to real artifacts and customer context.
These materials are intentionally **not included** in this repository.

You must supply them in your own working environment.

---

## 1. Customer Evidence

**Examples**
- Call transcripts
- Support tickets
- Slack threads with customers
- Written feedback or escalations

**Purpose**
Grounds the problem in the user’s actual language.
Prevents hypothetical or abstract problem framing.

**Quality Bar**
- Direct quotes preferred
- Multiple customers > single anecdote
- Evidence of frequency or impact

**If Missing**
The system will explicitly ask you to confirm you do not have this data before proceeding.

---

## 2. Personas

**Examples**
- Primary buyer persona
- Primary operator persona
- Secondary stakeholders

**Purpose**
Ensures requirements are tied to real roles, incentives, and constraints. Get these from your marketing team. 

**Quality Bar**
- Clear responsibilities
- Decision power vs execution responsibility
- Success metrics for the role

**If Missing**
You must define provisional personas before drafting any PRD sections.

---

## 3. Product Strategy / Positioning

**Examples**
- Strategy decks
- Internal positioning docs
- Target customer profiles

**Purpose**
Prevents off-strategy features and local optimization.

**Quality Bar**
- Clear target buyer
- Explicit non-goals
- Differentiation logic

**If Missing**
Expect frequent challenges around prioritization and scope.

---

## 4. Ideal PRD Example (Optional but Strongly Recommended)

**Examples**
- A past PRD that worked well
- A document that engineering trusted

**Purpose**
Sets expectations for depth, tone, and rigor.

**If Missing**
The system will default to conservative, detail-heavy PRD structures.

---

## 5. Screenshots & Visual References

Screenshots are a required input for grounding discussion in real user experience.
They are used to illustrate workflows, friction points, and proposed improvements.

### Types of Screenshots

**Current-State Experience**
- Existing product UI
- Where users get confused, slowed down, or blocked

**Desired-State Experience**
- Conceptual mocks or wireframes
- Rough UX sketches

**Reference Screenshots (Optional)**
- Publicly accessible competitor or adjacent-product UIs
- Used only to illustrate workflow or UX patterns
- Not to imply feature parity or endorsement

### Purpose

Screenshots help:
- make workflow pain concrete
- avoid abstract or purely textual reasoning
- align product, design, and engineering early
- pressure-test assumptions about usability and flow

### Quality Bar

- Screenshots should be clearly labeled (what, where, why it matters)
- Prefer flows over isolated screens

### Boundaries

- Do not include customer environments
- Do not include proprietary or internal-only UIs
- Do not include non-public competitor screenshots
- Do not rely on screenshots without written explanation
